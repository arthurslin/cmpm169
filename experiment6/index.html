Experiment 6 - Movie Hacking

Imitate

For this assignment, I wanted to imitate the feel that people get from watching a hacker in a movie. I think that a lot of movies have really goofy hacking scenes so I wanted to take my play on that. Here is some inspiration.

Integrate

For this step, I experimented with javascript chars and randomized them to get random ascii characters. Using this line of code: chat(random(32,126)) I was able to get any of the possible characters that are printable.

Not really what I wanted but I still needed to finely tune my script more. I decided to try a different approach and make the letters generated separately to get the aesthetic I was looking for. Innovate

This wasn’t very hacker-like so I wanted to make the text have meaning. I put 0s and 1s in to represent binary and to make it feel like the machine is reading the code. I thought long and hard about this and I realized that this is way too generic, and it didn't fit the vision that I wanted to take for this project. I wanted it to represent movie hacking and embrace the goofy factor of it. I changed up the vocabulary and took my own spin on it.

Reflection

I really enjoyed working on this assignment because I loved the mysteriousness of hackers when I was a kid. I thought that it was fascinating how people were able to understand computers when I thought that they were extremely complex. Now that I have studied computer science more, I started to understand a lot more about computers. Whenever I watch a movie with hacking in it, I often find a lot of the scenes to be really silly, especially older movies. Overall, the final product was really satisfying because it encompasses what I wanted to build for this experiment.
