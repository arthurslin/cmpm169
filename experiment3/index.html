Experiment 3 - Spiral Explosion

Imitate

I was on openprocessing.org and I saw this really cool sketch of a spiral that looked like waves. I thought it was pretty cool that a lot of art could be generated from spirals. I tried to imitate this, and I wanted to learn how to make a simple spiral. Here is my spiral. Which created a cool emergent X on the circle it generated. https://openprocessing.org/sketch/2050767 - Spiral

Integrate

I was thinking of integrating the spiral into a different shape using noise. I wanted to generate rippling effects on my canvas. I experimented with noise, but I think that this just kind of looked like a messy spiral. Maybe it looks like a rose, but I think it looks like a tumbleweed. I thought more about what I wanted, and I realized I can modify this chaotic behavior to make something, somewhat like this explosion. https://openprocessing.org/sketch/1732895 - Explosion

Innovate

I modified the noise function and wanted to see where I could take this. This time I ended up with a hairball. I think the points are straying too far from each angle, so I realized that I would have to take a different approach. So, I ended up using a p5js map, with target range being 0 to 2pi degrees. This is what I was looking for! I utilized the spiral to make an emergent explosion. I wanted to make it have nicer colors, instead of being just red.
